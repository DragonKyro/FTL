# Setting — The Collapse Era

> *They left us the doors and forgot to leave the keys.*
> — fragment recovered from a Choral Shrine, attribution unknown

## The short version

Ten thousand years ago a galactic-scale civilization — the **Vyrnari** —
vanished from the universe in a single instant. They left behind:

- the **Helix Gates**, a network of FTL conduits stitching the galaxy
  together;
- the **Crèche Worlds**, terraformed planets each engineered as a
  biosphere for one of dozens of "uplift" species;
- the **Echo**, a sub-frequency in subspace where the dead Vyrnari's
  thoughts can still, sometimes, be heard.

For ten millennia their children inherited the galaxy. They built empires
in the corridors the Vyrnari opened, fought wars with weapons the Vyrnari
left half-finished, prayed at shrines whose meaning no one remembers.

For ten millennia, the Gates worked. They no longer do.

In the last forty years, Gates have begun to **fail**, one by one. A
backwater system goes dark. A trade lane closes without warning. A
recovery fleet sent to investigate is never seen again. There are now
**three hundred sealed Gates** between the Inner Spiral and the Outer
Verge, and the rate is increasing.

This is the **Collapse**. No one agrees what it is or who is causing it.
Everyone agrees that within a generation, the galaxy will be unrecognizable.

The player begins as the captain of a small ship — a courier, a smuggler,
a refugee, an idealist, a defector — caught in the middle of all of it.

## The central question

> **Why are the Helix Gates failing, and what does it cost to save them?**

Every faction has an answer. Most of the answers are wrong. Some are
*also* right. The player's job is to live long enough to find out, and
then to decide what to do about it.

## The era's three pressures

### 1. The Collapse itself

Gates fail unpredictably. A working route can go silent overnight. Cargo,
news, fleets, families are stranded. Currency stops circulating where it
used to flow. Refugees from sealed systems pour through the remaining
arteries. Entire faction-borders are being redrawn in months, not decades.

### 2. The Outerlight

Something comes through failing Gates. Not always — most failures are
silent — but often enough to be the deepest, most denied open secret in
the galaxy. Ships disappear with their crews intact-but-empty. Whole
stations report a *light that isn't light* before going dark.

Some say the Vyrnari sealed the Gates *from this side* ten thousand years
ago to keep something out. Some say the Vyrnari simply locked the doors
when they went, and now those locks are aging. Some say *we* are doing
this — that the Lattice, or the Unmade, or the Choral Order's ritual
listening, is what cracks the Gates open.

The Outerlight is the final-boss thread. The player will not understand
it on first run. They are not supposed to.

### 3. The post-Vyrnari power vacuum

For ten thousand years the galaxy organized itself around the assumption
that the Gates would always work and the Vyrnari ruins would always be
quiet. Both assumptions are failing at once.

- The **Consilium of Stars** (the dominant pluralist federation) is
  fraying at the edges as its colonies are cut off.
- The **Iron Concordat** (a militarist breakaway) argues the Consilium
  failed and only strength will save what remains.
- The **Mercatile Verge** (the merchant guild controlling the Gates)
  charges higher tolls as supply collapses, deepening resentment.
- The **Choral Order** (mystics who listen to the Echo) splits between
  those who say the Vyrnari are calling for help and those who say they
  are calling for the End.
- Every other faction (see [factions/](factions/)) is positioning for
  what comes after.

## How this shapes the game

- Sectors are *failing*. Travel routes shift mid-run. Some beacons go
  dark and must be replaced. Late-game runs reach regions where most
  Gates are sealed and travel becomes harrowing.
- Factions overlap geographically. A single sector can have Consilium
  patrols, Black Vein smugglers, Choral pilgrims, and a Drift Commune
  ark all at once.
- The **storylines** (see [storylines.md](storylines.md)) are how a run
  picks a *thesis* about the Collapse and follows it through. Different
  theses give different ship unlocks and different final-boss
  configurations.
- The **final boss** is configured by your storyline. The default
  enemy is the Iron Concordat dreadnought *Throne of Ash* — but a
  player who walked the Choral path may instead face a reawakened
  **Quarantine Warden Prime**, a player who walked the Lattice path
  may face *their own uploaded mirror*, and a player who walked the
  Whisperborn/Unmade path may face **the Outerlight itself**.
