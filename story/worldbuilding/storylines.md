# Storylines — the twelve playable arcs

A *storyline* is a chain of 4–6 events the player can pursue across a
run, ending in a unique reward: usually a **new playable ship** and a
**permanent boon at the final boss fight**.

Storylines are *opt-in*. A player can ignore all of them and still
beat the game on the default path (Iron Concordat *Throne of Ash*).
A player can pursue *one* storyline per run — they are too long, and
too sector-specific, to stack. Completing a storyline once permanently
unlocks the associated ship for future runs.

Some storylines also **change the final boss**. A player on the Choral
Order path may end the run facing a reawakened **Warden Prime** rather
than the Concordat dreadnought. A player on the Lattice path faces
their own uploaded mirror. A player on the Whisperborn / Unmade path
faces *the Outerlight itself.* Different paths, different endings.

Each storyline below specifies:

- **Faction** — who the storyline orbits.
- **Entry trigger** — how it begins in a run.
- **Key beats** — the 4–6 events that comprise it.
- **Branching choices** — major decisions inside the arc.
- **Ship unlock** — the ship added to your shipyard on completion.
- **Final-boss boon** — the run-ending advantage.
- **Difficulty** — relative to a default run.

## 1. The Choral Order Pilgrimage

**Faction:** Choral Order. **Difficulty:** medium.

**Entry trigger.** At any Choral shrine beacon, accept a First-Speaker's
sealed commission to find the *Seven First-Tone Shrines*.

**Key beats.**

1. Receive the first three shrine locations (Choral starting hint).
2. Find Shrine I — defended by an Unmade ambush. Defeat or sneak past.
3. Find Shrine II — derelict. A Whisperborn fragment hides aboard; recruit
   or evacuate them.
4. Find Shrine III — held by a Verge toll station that won't grant access.
   Bribe, sneak, or fight.
5. Receive the next four shrine locations from a Choral elder. Re-consecrate
   them in sequence (the order matters; mis-sequencing aborts the
   storyline).
6. Final shrine triggers a Warden Prime activation. *(Now your final boss
   is the Warden Prime.)*

**Branching choices.** Whether to recruit or evacuate the Whisperborn (the
Whisperborn becomes a crewmate if recruited and unlocks dialogue branches
in the Vyrnari ruins). Whether to share Echo fragments with the Choral
elder, the Consilium Ninth Bureau (cash reward, faction rep tradeoff), or
no one (keep them yourself, possible later quest).

**Ship unlock.** **Echo-Lensed** — small, fast, sensor-bonus hull with a
shrine-aligned reactor that recharges 10% faster in Choral sectors.

**Final-boss boon.** Start each phase of the Warden Prime fight with **one
weapon pre-charged.**

## 2. The Mhirsa Liberation

**Faction:** Mhirsa Compact (antagonist: House Andrachen-in-Exile).
**Difficulty:** medium-hard (Andrachen ambushes scale).

**Entry trigger.** Accept a Yokebreaker Squadron commission at any
Mhirsa-controlled beacon, or be approached by a Mhirsa courier after
witnessing an Andrachen front in any sector.

**Key beats.**

1. Track down an Andrachen front company through three breadcrumb events.
2. Raid the front's clearing-station; recover ledger evidence.
3. Use the ledger to locate a hidden Andrachen breeder-station.
4. Raid the breeder-station. **Find civilian Andrachen non-combatants
   aboard.** Choose:
   - Free the bred occupants, hand the civilians over to Mhirsa justice.
   - Free the bred occupants, evacuate the civilians to a neutral port
     (Mhirsa rep hit, Consilium rep gain).
   - Burn the station entire (Mhirsa rep gain, Choral rep hit, *one
     crewmate quits if they're a Choral pilgrim or Splice*).
5. Hunt the Senior Auditor responsible. Three of the encounters can be
   skipped via Verge bribes; doing so reroutes the storyline through
   Verge complicity.
6. Confront the Senior Auditor in a final standoff.

**Branching choices.** See beat 4. Also: whether to spare the Senior
Auditor in exchange for the *complete Andrachen ledger* (cost: Mhirsa
favor; benefit: massive scrap & permanent map intel).

**Ship unlock.** **Yoke-Breaker** — Mhirsa-built warship with +1 starting
combat skill on all crew, integrated boarding teleporter.

**Final-boss boon.** Crew **immune to mind control** for the entire boss
fight.

## 3. The Verdantis Pilgrim Path

**Faction:** Verdantis. **Difficulty:** hard (self-imposed constraints
limit your loadout).

**Entry trigger.** At a Verdantis garden beacon, take the
Boughspeaker's vow.

**Constraint.** For the rest of the run you may not:

- Install any cybernetic upgrade or augment marked "cybernetic" or
  "lattice-derived."
- Recruit Spliced or Hollow crew.
- Accept Andrachen-touched cargo (which you discover via Yssari
  intuition or events).

**Key beats.**

1. Visit Crèche World *Pellrun-Yth*. Receive the Boughspeaker's blessing
   and the first ritual key.
2. Visit Crèche World *Vol-Karran*. Survive a high-gravity orbital event;
   recruit a Ferran pilgrim.
3. Visit Crèche World *Tholai*. Witness an Yssari binding rite; *cannot
   use fire weapons in this sector*.
4. Visit Crèche World *Inrith*. Meet a Pulsar-Drowned Vor and receive a
   rare radiation-tolerance graft (the *one* "bio-modification" the path
   permits, because Verdantis canon considers it restoration, not change).
5. Visit Crèche World *Sothan-Vell*. The Drevant gift you a *Living Hull*
   sapling.
6. Return to *Pellrun-Yth*. Grow the sapling into your ship.

**Branching choices.** Whether to accept the Vor graft (refusing
preserves vow purity for an even bigger boon, but the rest of the run
gets meaningfully harder).

**Ship unlock.** **Living Hull** — semi-organic ship that regenerates
1 HP per jump and grants crew +25% medbay healing.

**Final-boss boon.** Your ship's hull **slowly regenerates throughout the
final fight** (+2 HP per phase). If you refused the Vor graft, it's +4
HP per phase.

## 4. The Splice Initiation

**Faction:** Splice. **Difficulty:** medium (variable based on graft
choices).

**Entry trigger.** Visit a Splice Hall in any Outer-Verge station and
ask for an introduction.

**Key beats.**

1. The Splice surgeon gives you a *graft list* — eight species traits
   available to collect samples from.
2. Travel to events that let you collect samples (combat, diplomacy,
   exploration; each species has a different acquisition method).
3. Return to the Hall. Choose which grafts to install on which crewmates.
   *Each graft permanently mutates the crewmate.* Some combinations
   cause secondary mutations (the Halls explain in detail).
4. Defend the Hall from a Verdantis-led mob attack.
5. Defend the Hall from a (more dangerous) Unmade bombing attempt.
6. Graduate as a Spliced captain.

**Branching choices.** Which grafts. Whether to *over-graft* a single
crewmate (more power, faster XP loss). Whether to defend the Hall with
lethal force or non-lethal (Choral rep gain on non-lethal).

**Ship unlock.** **Patchwork** — composite hull built from six donor
ships; starts with three Spliced crew (free trait stacking).

**Final-boss boon.** Each boss subsystem you destroy grants a
**temporary +1 weapon damage** for the rest of the fight.

## 5. The Iron Concordat Crucible

**Faction:** Iron Concordat. **Difficulty:** hard (combat-heavy).

**Entry trigger.** Accept a Concordat Marshal's commission at any
Concordat-controlled beacon.

**Key beats.**

1. Survive seven hostile combat encounters consecutively *without
   fleeing*. (Fleeing voids the storyline.)
2. After encounter 3, you are given the option to recruit a Karrukai
   shock-troop. (Accepting changes your crew dynamics; Karrukai may
   ignore orders 10% of the time.)
3. After encounter 5, a Choral pilgrim ship hails you for help. Helping
   them costs you reputation with the Concordat and voids the storyline
   *unless* you do it cleverly (a high-skill timing puzzle).
4. After encounter 7, you are inducted into the Marshal-Tribunal's *Iron
   Crucible* — a private squadron of the toughest captains.
5. As your final qualifier, you must *capture* (not destroy) a smaller
   Concordat-designated rebel ship and bring its captain alive to a
   Concordat dock.

**Branching choices.** Whether to recruit the Karrukai. Whether to help
the Choral pilgrim (almost certainly voids the storyline; almost
certainly the right thing to do). Whether to capture or kill the rebel
captain (kill: faster, *Throne of Ash* ending is harder. Capture:
Marshal rewards you with the final-boss boon's strongest variant).

**Ship unlock.** **Iron Lance** — heavy Concordat hull with extra reactor
capacity (10 baseline) and reinforced shields.

**Final-boss boon.** Start the boss fight with **shields fully
pre-charged.** If you captured the rebel captain alive, also start with
**one cloaking activation banked.**

## 6. The Drift Commune Way

**Faction:** Drift Communes (antagonist: Mercatile Verge).
**Difficulty:** medium (slower pace, restricted route).

**Entry trigger.** Dock with any Drift Commune ark and accept their
*Long-Burn Compact*.

**Constraint.** You may not dock at any Mercatile Verge toll station for
the rest of the run. (You can fight your way past them, but cannot pay
the toll.)

**Key beats.**

1. Establish trust with three Communes by completing one task each (a
   delivery, a defense, a mediation).
2. Charter your first long-burn jump (skip a Verge Gate via Commune
   tug).
3. Mediate a dispute between two Communes over Unmade infiltration
   accusations. Choose sides carefully; the wrong choice closes
   future Commune ports.
4. Help expel — or shelter — an Andrachen Auditor uncovered on a
   Commune (Mhirsa rep tradeoff).
5. Be initiated as a *Commune-Outrider* by the assembly of the largest
   neutral Commune.
6. Reach the final sector via Commune long-burn — bypassing the entire
   Verge-controlled final approach.

**Branching choices.** The Andrachen dilemma. Whether to publicly
expose the Verge's indentured-labor toll scandal (Commune rep gain,
Verge rep collapse, sector access changes).

**Ship unlock.** **Ark-Wing** — civilian-converted Commune outrider with
+50% cargo capacity (event drops scale up) and +1 fuel regen per jump.

**Final-boss boon.** **+2 fuel and +20 scrap** at the start of every
final-boss phase (resource cushion).

## 7. The Vellis-Lirath Tragedy

**Faction:** Twin Houses (and Captain Iolen). **Difficulty:** medium.

**Entry trigger.** Accept *either* a Crown's Lance (Vellis) or a White
Cipher (Lirath) contract.

**Key beats.**

1. Run two intel-gathering missions for your chosen house against the
   other.
2. Discover that *both contracts target the same person* — Captain
   Iolen Vellis-Lirath. The reveal can be early (if you're paying
   attention) or late (if you're not).
3. Iolen contacts you, offering to *triple your pay* if you reverse
   sides and protect them. Choose:
   - **Continue with the original house.** Hunt Iolen.
   - **Switch to Iolen's protection.** Both houses are now hostile.
   - **Play double-agent.** A tightrope; if you fail a stealth roll,
     you're at war with everyone.
4. Survive an ambush by the *other* house's hunters.
5. Reach Vassirin. A final confrontation depends on path:
   - **Loyal-house path.** Capture or kill Iolen. House wins. The
     compact is voided; civil war ends in your house's victory.
   - **Iolen-protection path.** Smuggle Iolen off-world and stage a
     dramatic public renunciation that *finally* sticks. Both houses
     are humiliated; the compact is reformed under a new generation.
   - **Double-agent path.** Broker an actual reconciliation between
     the houses, with Iolen as the reluctant cornerstone. Hardest
     ending. Only path that *recruits Iolen* as a crewmate.

**Ship unlock.** **Loyal-house path** unlocks **Crown's Gambit**
(Vellis) or **Cipher's Edge** (Lirath) — both are precision-strike
hulls with strong manning bonuses. **Iolen-protection path** unlocks
**Half-Crown** (Iolen's own ship). **Double-agent path** unlocks
**Half-Crown** *and* recruits Iolen as starting crew.

**Final-boss boon.** Vellisian/Lirathi crew on the Loyal path get
+25% manning bonus. Iolen as crew on the Double-agent path is *itself*
the boon: Iolen's manning grants +2 evasion to any system they're on.

## 8. The Lattice Offer

**Faction:** Lattice. **Difficulty:** medium (philosophical, not
combat-heavy).

**Entry trigger.** Accept a Lattice contact-thread at any major beacon.

**Key beats.**

1. Visit a Lattice node. Tour the substrate. (Optional dialogue: the
   Speaker-thread explains the Lattice's reasoning, history, and
   ethics. Multi-hour event.)
2. Accept a contract to escort a Lattice thread between nodes. Survive
   Concordat hunter-killer ambushes.
3. Witness a crewmate suffer a near-fatal injury. The Lattice offers
   them upload. Choose:
   - **Refuse the upload.** Crewmate dies (or recovers slowly).
   - **Accept the upload.** Crewmate becomes a Hollow, returns to crew
     with no biological needs but +2× ion vulnerability.
4. Investigate a Lattice node where the Involuntary-Upload Faction
   has taken over. Choose:
   - Side with the mainstream Lattice and purge the faction.
   - Side with the faction and *gain a darker storyline branch*.
5. Be initiated as a *Lattice Ally*.

**Branching choices.** Major: the involuntary-upload decision shifts
the storyline's ending boon between a *bright* and *dark* version.

**Ship unlock.** **Substrate** — Lattice-built synthetic-friendly hull
that ignores oxygen entirely (crew never suffocate) and houses Hollow
crew natively.

**Final-boss boon.** **Pre-disable one of the boss's systems** at the
start of the fight (you choose which: weapons-1, shields, engines,
cloaking, etc.). On the *dark* (involuntary-upload) path, you can
pre-disable two.

## 9. The Quarantine Wardens

**Faction:** Quarantine Wardens (antagonist). **Difficulty:** very
hard.

**Entry trigger.** Acquire a Vyrnari relic from any source (Black
Vein dealer, salvage, Andrachen front).

**Constraint.** Wardens begin actively hunting you. They will appear
in increasing numbers across sectors.

**Key beats.**

1. Survive a Warden first-contact engagement. (Most ships die here.)
2. Acquire a *second* relic that *deflects* Warden attention — the
   relic-bait technique (story-introduced via Black Vein rumor).
3. Use the bait to study Warden movement patterns. Document an
   anomaly: at least two Wardens act on conflicting orders.
4. Locate a *Warden in repair* — a wounded Warden in a derelict state,
   undergoing self-repair. (Players who skip this beat lose the
   storyline's most powerful reward.)
5. *Salvage from the Warden* without triggering full reactivation. (A
   skill-and-luck event chain; failure means a Warden Prime ambushes
   you immediately.)

**Branching choices.** Whether to attempt step 4 at all. (Skipping it
gives a weaker reward; attempting and failing is catastrophic.)

**Ship unlock.** **Sealwalker** — small, fast hull built around
Warden-salvaged subspace-projection tech. Carries a one-shot Warden
*projection cannon* (cooldown is the entire run; use wisely).

**Final-boss boon.** **Wardens ignore you for the remainder of the
run** (including the final boss arena, where Wardens may otherwise
appear as a wild card). If you skipped step 4, the boon is reduced:
Wardens still ignore you in *most* of the run but appear during the
boss fight as wild cards (which can sometimes work in your favor).

## 10. The Black Vein Run

**Faction:** Black Vein Syndicate. **Difficulty:** medium (lower
direct combat, higher reputation pressure).

**Entry trigger.** Accept any Black Vein contract that includes the
word "no questions."

**Key beats.**

1. Run smuggling cargo through a Verge customs check. Choose: bribe,
   bluff, or fight through.
2. Defend a Cartel Chair's safehouse from a Mhirsa Yokebreaker raid.
   Choose your side carefully — Mhirsa rep will collapse.
3. Investigate a *slave-running crew* operating under the Black Vein
   banner. Choose to:
   - Report them to the Chair of Chairs (Korrin Vell), who *quietly
     removes them* (Black Vein rep stable, Mhirsa rep gain).
   - Hunt them yourself (Mhirsa rep gain, Black Vein rep loss).
   - Ignore them (Mhirsa rep loss, Black Vein rep gain).
4. Acquire and fence a Vyrnari relic. (Triggers Warden risk for the
   rest of the run.)
5. Become a Cartel Chair in your own right.

**Branching choices.** The slave-runner choice, primarily. Also: do
you fence the relic to a Black Vein buyer, an Andrachen front (more
money, faction rep collapse), or a Choral shrine (no money, large
Choral rep gain)?

**Ship unlock.** **Shrike** — Black-Vein-built hunter hull with +1
starting weapon slot and +20% projectile speed.

**Final-boss boon.** *Infamy*: at the start of the boss fight, a 30%
chance that smaller boss-section drones flee for one phase. Combined
with the Sealwalker boon (if both stories complete across multiple
runs), highly favorable.

## 11. The Whisperborn Awakening

**Faction:** Choral Order / Whisperborn (antagonist: Unmade and the
Outerlight). **Difficulty:** hard (story-rich, combat moderate).

**Entry trigger.** Start a run with a Whisperborn crew member (a
starting-ship option, or recruit one during the Choral Pilgrimage
storyline).

**Key beats.**

1. Your Whisperborn experiences a vision and gives you a *coordinate*
   that doesn't appear on any star map.
2. Travel to the coordinate. It is a *failing Helix Gate* — an
   Outerlight-active failure in progress.
3. Witness the Outerlight directly. *Your Whisperborn collapses.*
   Choose:
   - Retreat. Your Whisperborn lives, the storyline pauses.
   - Approach. Your Whisperborn *channels* the Outerlight briefly
     and translates what they hear. Permanent stat change to the
     Whisperborn.
4. Track three subsequent visions to three more Outerlight events.
   Each one offers a *fragment of understanding*: what the Outerlight
   *wants*, what the Vyrnari did, what is on the other side of the
   sealed Gates.
5. The Unmade discover what you're doing and hunt you. Survive their
   pursuit.
6. The final beat varies by your choices in beats 3 and 4:
   - **The Outerlight is what the Vyrnari were running from.** Fight
     it. (New final boss: the Outerlight itself, harder than any
     other final boss.)
   - **The Outerlight is the Vyrnari, returning, no longer
     themselves.** Bargain. (Different ending: dialogue-based final
     encounter, not a straight fight.)
   - **The Outerlight is the Vyrnari, returning, *still themselves*.**
     Welcome them. (Rare third ending; transforms the meta-game
     significantly.)

**Branching choices.** All major. Whisperborn awareness fragments
recombine differently depending on choices.

**Ship unlock.** **Echo-Veil** — Whisperborn-tuned hull that lets the
ship occasionally "phase" through one incoming projectile per fight.

**Final-boss boon.** During the final boss fight, you may spend
*story-points* (earned from understanding-fragments) to **undo a hit**.

## 12. The Unmade Heresy

**Faction:** Unmade (antagonist: Choral Order and everyone else).
**Difficulty:** hard (every other faction increasingly hostile).

**Entry trigger.** Read an Unmade pamphlet at a neutral beacon and
*accept* an offer to meet a recruiter.

**Constraint.** Choral Order rep collapses immediately and cannot be
recovered. Most other factions become increasingly hostile.

**Key beats.**

1. Meet the Unmade cell. They explain doctrine. Choose to commit or
   walk away (the latter ends the storyline). If you commit, your
   ship is ritually scarred (visual change for the rest of the run).
2. Bomb a Choral shrine. Most player crew refuse the order; those
   that refuse *quit the ship at the next port*. (You can stack the
   ship in advance with Splice or Argonite crew, who are more
   ideologically flexible.)
3. Hunt a Whisperborn for the Unmade. Choose: kill, capture, or
   *let them escape* (the last secretly maintains your Whisperborn-
   Awakening storyline option for a future run).
4. Sabotage a Verge Gate-restoration project. Triggers Warden activity
   in the sector permanently.
5. Meet the **Hollow Speaker**. (This is the only storyline that lets
   you meet them.) Learn their identity — which is, in nearly every
   playthrough, a *significant emotional and ideological blow* designed
   to test whether you stay committed.
6. Final beat: at the boss fight, the Unmade have arranged for an
   Outerlight Gate-failure to occur *during the fight*. You may:
   - **Fight the boss normally.** Standard ending.
   - **Welcome the Outerlight through.** *True dark ending.* The
     player wins the boss fight but loses everything else.

**Branching choices.** Every beat is a choice. Step 5 is the
storyline's emotional pivot.

**Ship unlock.** **Cinder-Crown** — ritually-scarred Unmade hull that
deals +1 damage per weapon (zealot bonus) but *cannot be unequipped*
once chosen (it stays scarred even when used in non-Unmade runs).

**Final-boss boon.** Once per final boss fight, you may **call upon
the Unmade fleet** for one strike (heavy damage but kills one of your
crew). On the *Welcome* ending, this is replaced by the Outerlight
arriving as an ally for the final phase.

## How storylines interact across runs

- Each completed storyline **permanently unlocks the associated ship**
  for the player's shipyard.
- Each completed storyline grants the player *meta-points* that can
  unlock alternate scenarios on future runs.
- Some storylines (*Quarantine Wardens*, *Black Vein Run*) can be
  *combined* with most other storylines in a single run if the
  player accepts the dual-constraints — high difficulty.
- *Whisperborn Awakening* and *Unmade Heresy* are mutually exclusive
  within a single run (both require committing to opposite ideologies).
- *Verdantis Pilgrim Path* and *Splice Initiation* are mutually
  exclusive within a single run for the same reason.
- *Lattice Offer* and *Verdantis Pilgrim Path* are mutually exclusive
  within a single run for the same reason.

The intent is that completing all twelve storylines (across many
runs) reveals the full picture of the Collapse Era and gives the
player every possible final-boss path.
