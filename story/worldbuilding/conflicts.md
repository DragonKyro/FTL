# Conflicts — the layered tensions of the Collapse Era

The Collapse is the *backdrop*. The conflicts in this document are what
the **player will actually be navigating**: political wars, ethical
schisms, ancient grudges, family feuds, religious heresies, economic
chokeholds. Each tier of conflict has at least one playable storyline
(see [storylines.md](storylines.md)) and a dedicated faction (or pair of
factions) whose grievances drive it.

The design rule is: **every faction's worldview is partly right.** The
player should be able to defend any side they've taken at the end of a
run. There are no clean heroes here.

---

## Tier 1 — Political: Rule by Council vs Rule by Sword

**Consilium of Stars vs Iron Concordat.**

The Consilium is the multi-species technocratic federation that has held
the galaxy together for eight centuries. It is bureaucratic, pluralist,
slow, increasingly authoritarian as the Collapse intensifies. Its core
worlds are wealthy; its outer worlds are being abandoned to save the core.

The Iron Concordat is the secession that says the Consilium *has failed
its outer colonies* and that only military discipline can carve a survival
out of the Collapse. It conscripts. It crushes dissent. It also actually
defends the colonies the Consilium abandoned, which is why people join.

**The lie at the heart of it:** both sides believe sacrificing the *other*
side's worlds is acceptable. Both are right that the Collapse demands
hard choices. Both are wrong about who should choose.

Player roles: take a Consilium commission, take a Concordat commission,
play double-agent, ignore both. Storylines: *The Iron Concordat Crucible*.

## Tier 2 — Economic: The Toll and the Smuggler

**Mercatile Verge vs Black Vein Syndicate.**

The Verge controls the Helix Gates that still work. They built artificial
stations at every Gate junction. They take tolls in scrap or in goods.
As the Collapse deepens, their tolls deepen with it. Some now demand
indentured labor as payment.

The Black Vein Syndicate runs the *un-tolled* paths — old smuggler
conduits, partial Gates, dangerous void-routes. They charge less and ask
no questions, and they also run kidnapping rings, salvage-piracy, and
sometimes-slave trade in Vyrnari relic markets.

**The lie:** the Verge says they keep civilization fueled. They do. They
also extort it. The Black Vein says they liberate trade. They do. They
also prey on the weak.

Player roles: pay tolls and profit honestly, run smuggler routes and lose
reputation with Consilium / gain with Black Vein, hunt pirates, *become*
pirates. Storylines: *The Drift Commune Way* (refuse the Verge entirely),
*The Black Vein Run* (full pirate playthrough).

## Tier 3 — Religious: The Listener and the Heretic

**Choral Order vs The Unmade.**

The Echo is real. Some species (especially the **Choir** and the
**Whisperborn**, see [species.md](species.md)) can hear fragments of the
dead Vyrnari thinking, dreaming, *crying out*. Most Echo-listeners hear
gibberish. A rare few hear coherent prophecy.

The **Choral Order** is the centuries-old monastic federation that has
catalogued every prophetic fragment ever heard. They believe the Vyrnari
are *calling for help* from beyond death and that the Gates can be
restored if the right rites are performed at the right shrines in the
right order.

The **Unmade** are heretics from the Choral Order. They claim that the
Vyrnari are not calling for help — they are *warning us to stop*. The
Vyrnari, the Unmade say, *deliberately* sealed the galaxy and went into
the dark to spare the universe what was coming. By trying to re-open the
Gates, the Choral Order is *finishing what the Vyrnari prevented*. The
only righteous act is to **complete the Collapse** — close every Gate,
seal every system, let the galaxy go dark and survive in silence.

**The lie:** both interpretations of the Echo are coherent. Neither is
falsifiable. And both produce believers willing to die for them.

Player roles: pilgrim, heretic, agnostic, opportunist. Storylines: *The
Choral Order Pilgrimage*, *The Unmade Heresy*, *The Whisperborn Awakening*.

## Tier 4 — Biological: What is a person?

**Verdantis vs Splice vs Lattice vs Hollow.**

The Vyrnari uplift program seeded the Crèche Worlds with **engineered
species**. Most current galactic peoples are descended from this program.
Now four philosophies fight over what to do with that inheritance:

- **The Verdantis** say: *the bodies we were given are sacred*. They
  refuse genetic editing, cybernetic implants, even some pharmaceuticals.
  Their culture is beautiful, rigid, and (their critics say)
  racially-pure-ist. They refuse to crew with Spliced or Hollow.
- **The Splice** say: *evolution is a tool, use it*. They graft traits
  across species lines. A Splice individual might have a Sapien body,
  Argonite respiration, Karrukai reflexes, and Loam regeneration. The
  Verdantis call them abominations. The Splice call the Verdantis cowards.
- **The Lattice** say: *bodies are containers. Upload.* They are a
  distributed AI-and-uploaded-mind collective that began as a Vyrnari
  subroutine and woke up. They offer immortality in their substrate.
  Most species find this horrifying. A few (the **Hollow**) accept.
- **The Hollow** are Lattice *defectors* — uploaded minds in synthetic
  bodies who left the substrate. They retain ego and memory. The Lattice
  considers them traitors. The Verdantis considers them abominations.

**The lie:** each is right that the *others* are dangerous. None is right
that *they* are not.

Player roles: build a crew along any of these lines, accept transformative
upgrades or refuse them. Storylines: *The Verdantis Pilgrim Path*,
*The Splice Initiation*, *The Lattice Offer*.

## Tier 5 — Slavery and reckoning

**Mhirsa Compact vs House Andrachen-in-Exile.**

Two centuries ago, House Andrachen ran the corporate hegemony of the Inner
Spiral. They bred a worker-species called the **Mhirsa** — ape-descended,
short-lived, strong, obedient (in theory). After two generations the
Mhirsa rebelled, won the *Slave Wars*, and dismantled the hegemony. The
Consilium recognized them as a sovereign people. The surviving Andrachen
fled into exile.

A century later, House Andrachen-in-Exile is a quietly powerful network
of information brokers, off-the-books financiers, and patient plotters.
They want their old empire back. They want their Mhirsa back. They are
willing to wait generations.

The Mhirsa Compact is the militarized federation the freed Mhirsa built.
They are paranoid, well-armed, and distrust *every* non-Mhirsa species
(including those who helped them in the war). They run hunter-killer
fleets that track down hidden Andrachen breeder-stations.

**The lie:** the Mhirsa are right to be paranoid. They have also become
the thing they fled — sometimes. The Andrachen are right that they were
defeated; they are wrong that they should be restored.

Player roles: ally with either, broker peace (very hard), or stay out.
Storylines: *The Mhirsa Liberation*.

## Tier 6 — Socio-economic: Land and Drift

**The Drift Communes vs the planet-dwelling galaxy.**

The **Drift Communes** are generations of people who have never set foot
on a planet. They were born and will die on slow-moving habitat-arks, some
older than the Consilium itself. Each Commune is internally egalitarian
and externally xenophobic. Some are Choral pilgrims. Some are pirates.
Most just want to be left alone.

The planet-dwellers (Consilium, Concordat, Verdantis, Mhirsa, Andrachen)
consider the Communes economically irrelevant and culturally strange.
The Communes consider the planet-dwellers *doomed* — anchored to one
world, dependent on Gates they don't control, defenseless against the
Collapse.

The Collapse is, in fact, vindicating the Drift Communes. They are the
only faction whose lifestyle is *uninterrupted* by Gate failures. This is
making them powerful in a way they have never been before. And they are
not, on the whole, prepared for it.

**The lie:** the Communes call themselves apolitical. They are not. The
planet-dwellers call the Communes irrelevant. They are not.

Player roles: travel by Commune jump-tugs to bypass the Verge, earn or
lose Commune favor, charter as a Commune outrider. Storylines: *The
Drift Commune Way*.

## Tier 7 — Romantic / Personal: The Tainted Heir

**The Twin Houses of Vellis and Lirath.**

On the homeworld of Vassirin, two ancient houses share a single throne.
The compact was sealed by a marriage two centuries ago — when the
houses interbreed, the throne is stable. When they don't, civil war.

Forty years ago a forbidden marriage produced a child the two houses
both claim and both reject — the **Tainted Heir**. The Heir is now a
captain in their forties, no fixed allegiance, a brilliant tactician
and a wreck of a person. Both houses fund mercenaries to capture them.
Both fund mercenaries to kill them. The Heir's existence is the reason
neither house can rule and neither will let the other.

This is the smallest, most personal conflict in the game. It is also
the most playable: family is universal, civil war is intimate.

**The lie:** there is no lie here. Just two people who loved each other,
two houses that couldn't bear it, and a child paying for it.

Player roles: accept missions from Vellis, from Lirath, from the Heir
themselves; broker the impossible reconciliation; *become* a captain
crewed by the Heir. Storylines: *The Vellis-Lirath Tragedy*.

## Tier 8 — Cosmic: The Door and the Watchers

**The Outerlight vs the Quarantine Wardens.**

The Vyrnari left **automated guardian ships** — the Quarantine Wardens
— that wake when they detect "wrong" tech activity near a Gate.
Encountering one is a death sentence in nine cases out of ten. Their
purpose is unclear: they sometimes attack ships using stolen Vyrnari
relics, sometimes attack ships *near* failing Gates, sometimes attack
*nothing in particular* before vanishing again.

The **Outerlight** is the thing the Vyrnari may have been guarding
against. It comes through failing Gates. It does not communicate. Ships
that meet it return crewed by *something else* or do not return at all.

The unanswered question: are the Wardens hostile to *us* because we
look like the Outerlight to them, or because they are *also* the
Outerlight, malfunctioning across a ten-thousand-year time gap?

This is the final-boss layer. The player will not understand it on the
first run. They are not supposed to.

Player roles: avoid Wardens, defy Wardens (very dangerous), commune with
Outerlight (Whisperborn/Unmade only). Storylines: *The Quarantine
Wardens*, *The Whisperborn Awakening*, *The Unmade Heresy*.

## How conflicts interact

These tiers do **not** form a clean hierarchy. They interleave:

- A Consilium captain (Tier 1) may pay a Mercatile Verge toll (Tier 2)
  to deliver Choral Order pilgrims (Tier 3) to a shrine that the Unmade
  (Tier 3) intend to bomb, on a route that crosses a Drift Commune ark
  (Tier 6) where the Tainted Heir (Tier 7) is hiding.
- An Iron Concordat war (Tier 1) creates refugees who become Splice
  experiments (Tier 4) under Andrachen patronage (Tier 5).
- A Lattice upload offer (Tier 4) is more attractive when your Gate
  home has been sealed (the Collapse) and a Warden Prime (Tier 8) is
  patrolling your only exit.

Encounters should *mix tiers freely*. A pirate ambush (Tier 2) might
turn into a religious dispute (Tier 3) when the pirates discover the
cargo is a Choral relic. A diplomatic meeting (Tier 1) might end in a
duel (Tier 7) when one envoy is a Vellisian and the other a Lirathi.
