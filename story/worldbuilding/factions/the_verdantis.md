# The Verdantis

> *The body the Vyrnari gave us is the temple they left us. Everything
> else is a betrayal.* — First Boughspeaker Eldress Inreth-Ven

## Identity & origin

The Verdantis are an ethical-religious movement, not a single species —
though their members are disproportionately from Crèche-World species
(Ferran, Loam, Yssari, and a particular lineage of Sapien who claim a
Crèche-World ancestry).

The Verdantis hold that the bodies the Vyrnari shaped through uplift
are *sacred objects*, untouchable by genetic engineering, cybernetic
implant, transhuman modification, or in some sects even aggressive
pharmacology. The body must be allowed to *be what it was made to be*.

They are old. The first Verdantis covenant predates the Consilium by
two centuries. They settled the Crèche World *Pellrun-Yth* and built
its biomes into a rigid, beautiful, *unchanging* civilization. They
expanded slowly — only to worlds where they could be the sole settlers,
because they will not share a biosphere with bio-modders.

Verdantis are led by the **Council of Boughs** — twelve Boughspeakers
chosen by consensus from each settled Crèche World, none of whom may
have been chosen twice. Currently led by First Boughspeaker
**Eldress Inreth-Ven**, a Loam, three hundred and eighty years old.

## Goals

- Preserve every Vyrnari-engineered body uncorrupted.
- Settle, claim, and ecologically restore additional Crèche Worlds.
- Resist all bio-modification movements (the Splice, the Lattice, the
  Hollow).
- Mourn — formally, ritually, continuously — the Vyrnari, who they
  consider the only *true* artists in galactic history.

## Methods

Cultural preservation. Diplomatic patience. Defensive militias (they
do not seek war; they will end any war that reaches their gardens).
Strategic intermarriage with Consilium aristocracy. Quiet purchase of
land titles on the worlds they want.

They do not run a navy. They charter Consilium escorts and pay in
Crèche-World produce, which is the most prestigious cuisine in galactic
high society. Both sides know they are bribing each other and consider
it polite.

## Internal tensions

- **Loam vs Sapien.** The Loam — functionally immortal fungal collectives —
  are the *paragons* of Verdantis ideology, since they regrow without
  surgery and live for centuries. Sapien Verdantis are theological
  juniors and resent it.
- **The Crèche-Restoration Project.** Some Boughs want to *re-seed*
  ruined Crèche Worlds with what they believe were the original Vyrnari
  ecosystems. Other Boughs consider this hubristic — a kind of bio-
  modification by another name.
- **The Splice Problem.** Verdantis communities have started *refusing
  emergency medical care* from off-world ships if the medics are
  Spliced. Several have died of treatable injuries. The Council of
  Boughs has not yet condemned this.
- **The Loam-Yssari Schism.** A minor but bitter sect of Yssari Verdantis
  argues that *telepathy itself* is a form of bio-modification and the
  Yssari must take vows of silence. Most Yssari Verdantis ignore them.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** allied through aristocratic intermarriage; protected
  by treaty.
- **Iron Concordat:** the Concordat sits in some Verdantis worlds and
  doesn't tax them. The Verdantis politely pretends not to notice.
- **Mercatile Verge:** trade partner. Verge ships ferry Verdantis
  produce and pay reduced tolls in return.
- **Choral Order:** respectful, theologically uneasy.
- **Unmade:** publicly denounced. Some Verdantis sympathy noted; the
  Council pretends otherwise.
- **Splice:** abominations. Will not crew with them. Sometimes refuses
  medical care from them.
- **Lattice:** also abominations, but more abstract.
- **Hollow:** also abominations.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** allies of convenience. Both factions oppose
  Andrachen breeding programs.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** historical enemies.
- **Drift Communes:** treated as alien but not hostile.
- **Black Vein:** mortal enemy of the bio-modder relic trade.
- **Wardens / Outerlight:** the Vyrnari's own. The Verdantis would
  never raise weapons against either, even in self-defense. (Many
  Verdantis ships have been lost to Wardens accordingly.)

## Where you find them in-game

- Verdantis gardens — beautiful, calming, free crew morale buffs on
  rest events.
- Pellrun-Yth and other Crèche Worlds.
- Refusal-encounters where a Verdantis ship will not interact with
  a Spliced crew member aboard your ship.

## Story hooks

- Be denied medical care for a Spliced crewmate by a Verdantis
  hospital. Choose what to do about it.
- Take a Verdantis envoy to a Choral Order shrine to debate doctrine.
- Investigate a Verdantis colony where Unmade sympathy has taken root.
- See the *Verdantis Pilgrim Path* storyline — hard-mode no-cybernetics
  run with the *Living Hull* ship as reward.
