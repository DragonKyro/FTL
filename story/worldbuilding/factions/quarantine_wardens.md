# The Quarantine Wardens

> *They burn anything that holds a relic. They burn anything that
> approaches a sealed Gate. They burn anything that uses a Vyrnari
> word at the wrong volume. Sometimes they burn nothing at all and
> are gone before sunrise. Plan for them like you plan for a comet.*
> — Verge captain's standing orders

## Identity & origin

The Quarantine Wardens are **automated Vyrnari guardian ships**.
The Vyrnari built them, ten thousand years ago or more. They have
been patrolling, dormant and waking, ever since.

Nobody knows how many there are. Estimates range from "fewer than
two hundred" to "tens of thousands distributed across non-overlapping
patrol arcs that no living being has surveyed." The Choral Order
has catalogued one hundred and sixty-three named Warden encounters
across recorded history. Most ended badly for the cataloguer.

A Warden is, physically: an obsidian-black hull, irregular geometry,
roughly the size of an Iron Concordat Throne-line dreadnought. No
running lights. No transponder. Weapons that no other ship can
match (some appear to *project damage across short subspace gaps*,
which is theoretically impossible). Shields that absorb almost
anything. Hulls that *self-repair* mid-fight.

A Warden has no observable bridge, crew, or escape pod. Several have
been destroyed in single engagements (always with severe attacker
losses). No Warden has ever surrendered or, as far as anyone knows,
attempted to communicate.

## Goals (best inferred from behavior)

- Detect and destroy "wrong" Vyrnari-tech activity near sealed Gates.
- Detect and destroy *unauthorized* use of Vyrnari relic technology
  anywhere.
- Patrol regions near *active* Helix Gates that show signs of
  destabilization.
- Possibly: prevent something specific from getting *through* Gates.
  This is the dominant Choral Order interpretation.
- Possibly: enforce some part of Vyrnari law no one alive can read.

Wardens *do not engage civilian ships* unless those ships are
carrying Vyrnari relics, transmitting Vyrnari-language signals, or
operating within a few thousand kilometers of a destabilizing Gate.
When they do engage, they almost always win.

## Methods

Engagement. Disengagement. *Vanishing*. A Warden in pursuit will
follow a target across multiple Gates if possible. A Warden that
chooses to disengage may do so mid-volley, without warning, leaving
its enemy intact (and confused, and grateful, and never quite trusting
again).

Wardens *appear* to use a form of Gate transit no other ship can.
They reach systems no current factional fleet can reach. The Choral
Order maintains theories about this. The theories are not falsifiable.

## Internal tensions

Whatever a Warden is, it does not internally tense. Each Warden
behaves coherently and as if it has a single will.

There is, however, *evidence of disagreement among Wardens.* Recorded
encounters include:

- Two Wardens converging on the same target and *peeling off in
  different directions* — one pursuing, one not.
- A Warden engaging a Vyrnari relic ship while a second Warden
  *observed and did not act*.
- A Warden destroying a Choral pilgrimage and another Warden, in the
  same system minutes later, *escorting a different Choral pilgrimage
  past a Gate.*

No one has a coherent model of why.

## Stance toward other factions

The Wardens have no diplomatic stance. They do not respond to hails.
The Choral Order has assembled the most thorough corpus of attempted
Warden communication (mostly Echo-channel) and the corpus is roughly
the size of one short book and entirely opaque.

Every faction *avoids* Wardens. Every faction has lost ships to
Wardens. Every faction has folk wisdom about Wardens that is mostly
wrong.

The Lattice is the only faction that has ever *briefly negotiated*
with an active Warden. The negotiation ended ambiguously, and the
Lattice does not discuss what was said.

## Where you find them in-game

- Near sealed Gates. Always a high-risk encounter.
- Anywhere a player is openly using Vyrnari relics.
- Following the player after they trigger a Warden engagement (very
  hard to lose).
- Sometimes: *helpfully* clearing a hostile fleet from the player's
  path and then leaving without engaging the player. (Rare. Story-
  significant when it happens.)

## Story hooks

- Acquire a Vyrnari relic, then realize Wardens have started showing
  up at your beacons. Decide what to do with the relic.
- Witness a Warden engage and destroy *another* Warden. (Extremely
  rare; story-significant.)
- Be saved by a Warden once. Be hunted by a Warden the next sector.
  Wonder why.
- See the *Quarantine Wardens* storyline — defy the Wardens, survive
  long enough to *salvage from one*, unlock the *Sealwalker* ship.
