# The Drift Communes

> *We do not need your Gates. We do not need your worlds. We carry our
> home with us, and we will carry it past your funeral.* — Drift
> assemblywoman on Consilium news feed, after declining federation
> membership for the seventh time

## Identity & origin

The Drift Communes are habitat-arks: enormous, slow, generations-old
station-ships that travel between systems under their own (sub-light)
power, never docking on planets. Each Commune is its own city-state,
internally egalitarian, externally insular. They predate the Consilium.
Some predate written galactic history.

Communes navigate by *intersystem long-burns* — multi-year voyages —
or, occasionally, by chartered Gate transit. They make money offering
their long-burn capacity to others: small ships dock with a Commune and
ride along for a fraction of the Verge toll, arriving wherever the
Commune is going next, on the Commune's schedule.

Each Commune is run by an **Assembly** of all adult residents,
deliberating in person on every major decision. This works because
Communes are small enough to do it (~5,000–40,000 residents on the
largest arks). Inter-Commune coordination is *minimal* and ad-hoc;
there is no Drift Federation.

## Goals

- Maintain Commune sovereignty. Refuse Consilium, Concordat, and Verge
  authority equally.
- Continue the long-burn voyages — for some Communes, on routes their
  ancestors set centuries ago.
- Survive the Collapse, which Communes have, in fact, been *more
  prepared for* than anyone realized.
- Take on whatever ideological flavor each Commune's Assembly votes
  for. (Some are Choral. Some are Unmade. Some are pirate. Most are
  none of these.)

## Methods

Self-sufficiency. Each Commune grows its own food, recycles its own
water, repairs its own hull, raises its own children. Communes *trade*
with stationary civilization but do not *depend* on it.

When a Commune fights — which is rare — it fights *defensively*, with
mounted weapons on the ark itself. They are slow, durable, and very
hard to assault. Several pirate fleets have learned this. So has, on
one memorable occasion, an Iron Concordat task force.

## Internal tensions

- **The Choral Communes vs the Unmade Communes.** A handful of
  individual Communes have, by Assembly vote, become Choral. A
  smaller number have become Unmade. These two kinds of Commune
  *avoid each other* at all costs. Inter-Commune assassinations
  have happened.
- **The Pirate Communes.** Several Communes have voted, by Assembly,
  to ally with the Black Vein and run smuggling. The Mercatile Verge
  has tried to *individually sanction* these Communes, which has
  driven other Communes (otherwise neutral) into solidarity with them.
- **The Outsider Question.** Some Communes accept *passengers* freely.
  Others strictly limit outsider presence. A few accept no outsiders
  at all and have been called "xenophobic." They reply that they're
  just Communes.
- **The Andrachen Shelter Problem.** Some Communes (unknowingly,
  mostly) shelter Andrachen Auditors. When this is revealed, the
  affected Commune's Assembly typically expels the Auditor — but
  several have voted to *keep* the Auditor for the financial benefit.
  This has poisoned Drift relations with the Mhirsa Compact.
- **Generational Reform.** Younger Commune residents increasingly
  want more inter-Commune coordination, easier outside travel, and
  formal alliances with stationary factions. Older residents — who
  remember why the Communes were *built* to be independent — resist.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** legally neutral; mutually disinterested.
- **Iron Concordat:** legally neutral; militarily uncomfortable.
- **Mercatile Verge:** *now* a competitor; previously irrelevant.
- **Choral Order:** Commune-by-Commune basis.
- **Unmade:** Commune-by-Commune basis, with significant Unmade
  penetration in some.
- **Verdantis:** mutually polite, mutually mystified.
- **Splice:** quietly accepting; Splice surgeons often shelter on
  Communes.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** historically allied (Communes sheltered Mhirsa
  rebels), currently strained by the Andrachen Shelter problem.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** problem (see above).
- **Lattice:** Communes generally refuse Lattice uploads. Some
  individuals defect anyway.
- **Hollow:** Communes welcome Hollow as legal persons; some Communes
  actively recruit Hollow as long-burn pilots.
- **Black Vein:** Commune-by-Commune basis. Some are Black Vein crews.
- **Wardens:** ancient hazard.
- **Outerlight:** vague. Most Communes refuse to discuss it.

## Where you find them in-game

- Habitat-ark beacons. Massive ships, slow, well-defended, full of
  trade and event opportunities.
- Drift jump-tug services that let you bypass Verge tolls (storyline-
  unlocked).
- Random encounters with smaller Commune fleets — supply ships,
  patrols, expeditions.

## Story hooks

- Pay a Commune for a long-burn ride and learn what's on the ark.
- Be approached by a Pirate Commune offering smuggler escort work.
- Help (or hinder) the Mhirsa Compact in expelling an Andrachen
  Auditor from a Commune that does not want to expel them.
- See the *Drift Commune Way* storyline — full anti-Verge run
  charting only Commune jumps, unlocks the *Ark-Wing* ship.
