# Consilium of Stars

> *We negotiate, we deliberate, we vote, and one day they will write that
> we lost the galaxy because of it. They will be wrong, and they will be
> right.* — Speaker-Plenary Henriette Vall, before the Sealing Crisis.

## Identity & origin

The Consilium is the dominant pluralist federation of the Inner Spiral
and most of the Mid-Verge. Founded eight hundred years ago after the
*Compact at Vassirin*, it codified multi-species citizenship, a unified
currency (the *consul-mark*), and a constitutional balance between
elected Speaker-Plenaries and a permanent technocratic civil service.
Its founding charter explicitly forbids any single species from holding
more than a third of senior office.

The Consilium worked, mostly, for centuries. Then the Helix Gates began
to fail.

## Goals

- Hold the Inner Spiral together by any peaceful means available.
- Maintain the multi-species charter (no single species supremacy).
- Restore Gate access without dependency on the Mercatile Verge.
- Suppress any faction whose ideology threatens the charter — by
  diplomacy where possible, by surveillance where not, by force where
  necessary.

## Methods

Diplomacy. Subsidy. Surveillance. Treaty. Bureaucracy as a defensive
weapon. The Consilium does not, by doctrine, *strike first* — and rarely
does in practice. When it strikes second, it strikes overwhelmingly.

Its navy, the **Plenary Fleet**, is technically defensive, well-equipped,
and conservatively commanded. Its intelligence arm, the **Ninth Bureau**,
is technically nonexistent and has very long memories.

## Internal tensions

- **Core vs Periphery.** Inner-Spiral worlds are wealthy and safe.
  Outer-Verge colonies are being abandoned to save them. The Periphery
  delegates know it. Many have quietly opened lines to the Iron Concordat.
- **Civilian vs Bureau.** The civil service has outlasted three Speaker-
  Plenaries and considers elected leadership a temporary inconvenience.
  Reform movements come and go; the Bureau outlasts them all.
- **The Halene Question.** Some core families argue that Halene reactor
  contributions make them irreplaceable. Others argue that no species
  should be *prized* over another under the charter. The argument is
  decades old and has not been resolved.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Iron Concordat:** legally a rebellion, practically an undeclared war.
- **Mercatile Verge:** uneasy partner. The Verge keeps trade flowing;
  the Consilium would prefer it didn't have a chokehold on the Gates.
- **Choral Order:** tolerated. Many Halene citizens are Choral. Some
  Bureau analysts believe the Order's prophecies are useful intelligence.
- **Unmade:** designated terrorist. Hunt orders standing.
- **Verdantis:** protected by treaty. Mostly leaves them alone.
- **Splice:** legally citizens. Socially mistrusted in core worlds.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** recognized sovereign nation. Diplomatic relations
  are cool but functional.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** designated criminal organization. Most of the
  Ninth Bureau's long-running operations target Andrachen networks.
- **Drift Communes:** legally neutral, practically irrelevant. This
  perception is changing fast.
- **Lattice:** unrecognized as a political entity. Privately feared.
- **Hollow:** legally persons. The Bureau watches them.
- **Wardens:** ancient automated hazard. Avoid.

## Where you find them in-game

- Inner Spiral and Mid-Verge sectors.
- Plenary Fleet patrols at most major beacons.
- Consilium customs stations at Gate crossings.
- Diplomatic missions, courts, refugee processing.

## Story hooks

- Carry sealed Consilium dispatches between worlds (paying but politically
  fraught).
- Take a Bureau commission: investigate an Unmade cell, an Andrachen
  network, a Lattice envoy.
- Help refugees from a Periphery sector being abandoned — to the
  Consilium's official discomfort and unofficial gratitude.
- Be caught between the Plenary and the Bureau when they disagree.
- See the *Iron Concordat Crucible* storyline for the Concordat's
  perspective on the same war.
