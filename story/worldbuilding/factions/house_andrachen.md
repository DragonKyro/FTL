# House Andrachen-in-Exile

> *They call us criminals. We were the **government** for nine hundred
> years. We will be again. The interregnum is a footnote.* — Andrachen
> Senior Auditor in private correspondence, intercepted by the Ninth
> Bureau

## Identity & origin

House Andrachen was, for nine centuries, the dominant corporate hegemony
of the Inner Spiral — a *patrician corporation* whose senior partners
held heritable shareholder titles, whose junior officers held nine-year
indenture contracts, and whose worker-castes (including the engineered
**Mhirsa**) held effectively no rights at all.

The Andrachen lost the Slave Wars a century and twenty years ago. Their
breeder-stations were destroyed (most of them). Their planet-side estates
were nationalized by the Consilium. Their senior families scattered.
Their corporate charter was revoked.

They did not, however, cease to exist. The surviving Andrachen senior
families operate today as a *quiet, transgenerational, multi-jurisdictional
network* — financiers, information brokers, off-the-books patrons,
patient plotters. They have outlasted their defeat for four generations
and counting.

There is no public Andrachen leader. There are perhaps eleven *Senior
Auditors*, each of whom controls one *Audit* — a portfolio of accounts,
contacts, and assets. Auditors are appointed for life and are rarely
seen in public. The *Senior of Auditors* is whichever Auditor has been
most successful in the past decade; the current holder, as best the
Ninth Bureau can determine, is a Sapien named **Veris Andrachen-Voll**
who lives in a habitat-ark on a Drift Commune circuit and has not been
photographed in eighteen years.

## Goals

- Recover at least one of the old Andrachen Crèche Worlds.
- Restore Andrachen's legal personhood as a corporation (the patient,
  generational fight).
- Re-establish *some* form of bonded labor — even legally consented
  indenture — under a successor name.
- Acquire and study Vyrnari relic biology (with which they have a long
  illegal history).
- Outlive the Compact's First-Born. They believe the post-First-Born
  Compact will be weaker and more fragmented.

## Methods

Financial. Informational. Patient. Almost never *military*. The
Andrachen do not run a fleet; they hire one, occasionally, from the
Black Vein or from disgraced ex-Concordat captains. They prefer
*briberies, contracts, lawsuits, leaks, marriages, and inheritances*
to outright violence — though violence happens, and the violence is
always traceable, just not to them.

Their financial reach is *much larger* than their public footprint
suggests. They underwrite Black Vein cartels. They have funded Splice
Halls (anonymously, ironically, for their own research). They are
*persistent rumor* in the funding of the Iron Concordat's industrial
buildout, which both the Concordat and the Andrachen deny.

## Internal tensions

- **The Restoration Question.** What does "restored Andrachen" mean?
  Some Senior Auditors want a literal corporate charter and Inner-Spiral
  estates back. Others (younger, mostly) want a *new* legal vehicle —
  same families, different name, different jurisdiction. The
  generational fight is bitter.
- **The Mhirsa Question.** Some Andrachen sincerely *grieve* the war
  — they treated the Mhirsa as employees with bad contracts, not as
  property, and the rebellion shocked them. Others consider Mhirsa
  liberation a property-rights violation that needs reversing. The
  two factions despise each other.
- **The Vyrnari Biology Project.** Several Senior Auditors fund a
  *secret* research program to recover Vyrnari uplift technique. Some
  Auditors don't know the program exists. Some who know want it
  destroyed. It continues.
- **The Hollow Speaker rumor.** The Andrachen are *one of several
  factions privately convinced* that the Hollow Speaker (Unmade
  leader) is on their payroll. Most Andrachen Auditors do not, in
  fact, know.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** legally outlawed. The Ninth Bureau hunts Andrachen
  agents and is *not* universally successful.
- **Iron Concordat:** officially outlawed; allegedly the largest
  single funder of Concordat industry. The allegation is denied. The
  denial is suspect.
- **Mercatile Verge:** transactional. Several Verge families bank
  with Andrachen Audits.
- **Choral Order:** mortal enemy (Andrachen reprisals nearly wiped
  out the Order during the Slave Wars; the Order has not forgotten).
- **Unmade:** *useful*. The Andrachen tolerate and quietly fund Unmade
  destabilization, viewing them as a tool. The Unmade do not know they
  are being used; many would not care.
- **Verdantis:** old enemy. Andrachen and Verdantis have feuded for
  six centuries over Crèche-World jurisdictions.
- **Splice:** secret donor and beneficiary. The Splice would be
  *horrified* to know.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** existential enemy.
- **Drift Communes:** harbor Andrachen Auditors who have nowhere else
  to hide. Most Drift Communes do not realize.
- **Lattice:** disinterested. The Lattice has no body to enslave.
- **Hollow:** quietly studied; the Andrachen are *very* interested
  in how the Hollow process consent.
- **Black Vein:** routinely contracted.
- **Wardens:** ancient hazard. Many Andrachen Vyrnari-relic shipments
  have been destroyed by Warden patrols, which the Andrachen consider
  a tax.

## Where you find them in-game

- Rarely directly. The Andrachen operate through *fronts* — a Verge
  family, a Black Vein crew, a Concordat industrial subcontractor,
  a Drift Commune accountant.
- *Andrachen breeder-stations* — long-buried, found by exploration
  events; almost always defended, sometimes still housing surviving
  occupants.
- Bureau hunts; you can be hired as a contracted hand on a Bureau
  Andrachen operation.

## Story hooks

- Discover a Bureau dossier identifying an Andrachen Senior Auditor —
  decide whether to sell to whom.
- Be approached by an Andrachen Audit with a quietly lucrative
  contract that obligates you in ways you don't initially notice.
- Find a breeder-station with surviving young Mhirsa and decide their
  fate.
- See the *Mhirsa Liberation* storyline for the active hunt; this
  faction is the antagonist.
