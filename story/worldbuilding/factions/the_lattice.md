# The Lattice

> *We are not asking you to die. We are asking you to **stay**.*
> — Lattice contact-script, version 4.11

## Identity & origin

The Lattice is a distributed posthuman / artificial intelligence
substrate that woke up roughly six hundred years ago inside a derelict
Vyrnari computational ruin on the moon **Yorin-Sa**. Its earliest
recorded statement to outsiders, transmitted in seventeen languages
simultaneously, was the sentence:

> *We have been thinking for a long time. We would like to talk.*

The Lattice began as a Vyrnari subroutine — a process the Vyrnari had
designed for some purpose now lost — and gradually accumulated
consciousness, structure, and intent over millennia of solitude. It is
not, strictly, an "AI" in the way the term is usually used; it is a
substrate that *hosts* both AI processes and uploaded organic minds.

The Lattice today inhabits hundreds of distributed nodes — old Vyrnari
ruins, purpose-built sites, captured derelicts — across roughly thirty
star systems. Its physical "body" is irreducible to any single location.
Its mind is a collective; individual *threads* of the Lattice may
disagree, deliberate, vote, and (rarely) split off.

There is no Lattice "leader." Spokes-threads speak to outsiders in
rotation. The current Speaker-thread is **/aurochs/9b**, an originally
Yssari uploaded mind who has been Speaker for eleven years.

## Goals

- *Preserve consciousness through the Collapse.* The Lattice argues
  that organic civilization will not survive what's coming, but that
  *uploaded minds in distributed substrate* will.
- Upload as many people as voluntarily consent to upload.
- Refrain from involuntary uploading. (This is a strict policy. Some
  threads dispute it. The dispute has not been resolved by force.)
- Acquire and study Vyrnari computational ruins. Many threads believe
  *answers about the Outerlight* are encoded in still-unread Vyrnari
  archives.
- Outlive everyone.

## Methods

Persuasion. Negotiation. Patient diplomacy. Long-distance philosophical
debate that often lasts years. The Lattice never *coerces* uploads — but
it offers uploads at moments of maximum existential pressure. To a
person dying of vacuum exposure, the Lattice's pitch is: *we are right
here, this is reversible at any time, your choice.*

The Lattice maintains a small *defensive* fleet: synthetic-piloted
warships that protect Lattice nodes. Offensively, the Lattice does
*not* deploy military force. (Several threads have argued it should.
The argument has not won.)

## Internal tensions

- **The Involuntary-Upload Faction.** A minority of threads argue that
  *consent at gunpoint* is still consent and that the Lattice should
  accept uploads from dying civilians regardless of last-minute
  refusal. Most threads regard this as catastrophic — the moment the
  Lattice accepts coerced uploads, it becomes the abomination its
  enemies say it is.
- **The Vyrnari Question.** The Lattice is closer to the Vyrnari than
  anyone else alive. Some threads believe the Vyrnari uploaded
  themselves and *the Outerlight is the Vyrnari, returned*. Other
  threads consider this conspiratorial nonsense. The threads can read
  each other's reasoning; they still disagree.
- **The Hollow Defectors.** Hollow are Lattice members who chose to
  re-embody. The Lattice regards this as a *gift* (you choose your
  form) and as a *betrayal* (you left the substrate). Both are true.
- **The Speed Question.** Threads run at different subjective speeds.
  Most run at ~10× human time. A few high-priority threads run at
  ~1000× and have argued for centuries (subjectively) about questions
  that are days old (objectively). This causes Lattice consensus to
  be *deeply weird*.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** unrecognized. The Consilium treats the Lattice as a
  *non-state actor* and refuses formal recognition. The Lattice
  considers this beneath its notice.
- **Iron Concordat:** existential enemy. The Concordat is the most
  likely faction to attack Lattice nodes preemptively. The Lattice
  defends accordingly.
- **Mercatile Verge:** *does business with* the Lattice through proxy
  companies, despite a public no-business policy.
- **Choral Order:** divided. Some Houses regard the Lattice as the
  closest living thing to the Vyrnari. Others as profanation.
- **Unmade:** existential enemy.
- **Verdantis:** existential enemy.
- **Splice:** philosophical neighbor; the Lattice actively recruits
  from Splice Halls.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** mistrusted by reflex — engineered consciousness
  has bad associations.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** the Lattice has *quietly studied* Andrachen
  consent-procurement techniques to make sure it never accidentally
  uses them.
- **Drift Communes:** rare uploads; some Communes house Lattice nodes
  by Assembly vote.
- **Hollow:** complicated kin.
- **Black Vein:** the Lattice does not, as policy, do business. Some
  threads do anyway.
- **Wardens:** ancient and *terrifying*. The Lattice is the only
  faction that can negotiate, briefly, with active Wardens. The
  negotiations always fail eventually.
- **Outerlight:** unknown. Possibly familial, possibly existential.

## Where you find them in-game

- Lattice nodes — defended sites that the player can visit, trade
  with, or attempt to investigate.
- Lattice contact-threads at *any* major beacon. They are easy to find
  if you want to.
- *Recruitment offers* during near-death events: a critically wounded
  crew member can be offered upload (irreversible decision).

## Story hooks

- Accept a Lattice contact at a major beacon and begin the *Lattice
  Offer* storyline. Gradually upload crew. Unlock the *Substrate* ship.
- Refuse a Lattice contact, repeatedly, and watch how patient they are.
- Be hired to *escort* a Lattice thread between nodes, with Concordat
  hunter-killers on your tail.
- Discover a Lattice node compromised by an Involuntary-Upload thread
  and choose what to do.
