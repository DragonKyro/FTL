# Iron Concordat

> *The Consilium passed a resolution about Outpost Vela. We sent the
> Eighth Fleet. The colony is still ours. The resolution is now ours
> too.* — High Marshal Doren Karvath

## Identity & origin

The Iron Concordat is a militarized federation of Outer-Verge worlds
that seceded from the Consilium twenty-three years ago, after the
*Vela Abandonment* — when the Consilium quietly decided not to defend
twelve outer colonies whose Gates were failing.

The Concordat began as the *Vela Auxiliary*, a volunteer fleet raised
by colonial governors. When the Consilium ordered them to disband, they
didn't. The secession was formalized at the **Treaty of Iron**, signed
on the hull of a scrapped Plenary cruiser.

The Concordat is run by a *Marshal-Tribunal*: seven Marshals elected by
the line officers, each holding portfolio for one war-front or province.
The current High Marshal is Doren Karvath, a Sapien colonial-born veteran
of three Gate-Sealing crises.

## Goals

- Hold every colony the Consilium abandoned.
- Build a fleet capable of forcing the Consilium to negotiate as an
  equal, not a parent.
- Survive the Collapse by industrial output and ruthless training.
- *(Doctrinally unstated, but widely understood:)* eventually subsume
  or replace the Consilium.

## Methods

Industrial conscription. Veteran-officer culture. Heavy doctrine — big
ships, redundant systems, lots of armor, fewer specialists. Their flagship
class, the **Throne-line dreadnought**, is the most feared warship in
known space.

The Concordat *does not negotiate from weakness*, ever, as policy. They
will refuse beneficial deals if the framing implies subordination.

## Internal tensions

- **Conscription Question.** The colonial-born line officers fought the
  secession and remember why. The newer conscripts (raised under Concordat
  draft law) sometimes resent serving in a war they didn't choose.
- **Karrukai shock-troop program.** Conscripting Karrukai as boarders is
  legal but controversial; line officers see them as a battlefield
  liability that scores victories.
- **Marshal vs Marshal.** Seven Marshals, seven war fronts, seven
  ambitions. Karvath is High Marshal because the others can't agree on
  her replacement.
- **The Andrachen Money.** Persistent rumor: the Concordat's heavy
  industrial base is partly funded by Andrachen-in-Exile loans. Karvath
  denies it. Some Marshals are deeply uneasy.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** war.
- **Mercatile Verge:** transactional. Pay the toll, sail through. Resented
  but tolerated.
- **Choral Order:** tolerated. Some line officers are Choral pilgrims.
- **Unmade:** designated terrorist; field execution authorized.
- **Verdantis:** untouched; the Verdantis sit inside Concordat space
  without paying tribute. The Concordat has decided not to make this a
  problem.
- **Splice:** widely tolerated. Many Spliced soldiers serve.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** correct, distant, sometimes-allied. The Concordat
  and the Mhirsa fought the Andrachen together in the Slave Wars. They
  remember. The Andrachen-in-Exile rumor strains this.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** officially designated criminal. Privately,
  inconvenient.
- **Drift Communes:** ignored. Mistake.
- **Lattice:** considered an existential threat.
- **Hollow:** rare in Concordat service; viewed with discomfort.
- **Wardens:** ancient hazard. Treated like weather.

## Where you find them in-game

- Outer-Verge sectors.
- Concordat customs at every contested Gate.
- War-front zones: high enemy density, hard fights, valuable salvage.
- *Recruiting offices* in Verge stations actively trying to hire the
  player's crew.

## Story hooks

- Take a Concordat commission: escort a relief convoy to a war-front
  colony. Pays well. Permanent rep change.
- Refuse a Concordat boarding inspection and learn what *that* costs.
- Hunt an Unmade cell under Concordat letters of marque.
- Discover the Andrachen-loan rumor is true and decide what to do with it.
- See the *Iron Concordat Crucible* storyline — full Concordat playthrough,
  ending in a duel with a Throne-line dreadnought.
