# The Splice

> *They say my body is not the body I was given. They are correct. It
> is the body I made. That should be the highest praise a body can earn.*
> — Splice artisan Yenra Vell-Mok, of three lineages

## Identity & origin

The Splice are not a species — they are a *culture* and an *art form*.
Anyone, of any species, can become Spliced. The Splice movement teaches
the deliberate, considered grafting of traits from one species onto
another's body: an Argonite respiration system installed in a Sapien
chest cavity, Karrukai reflex bundles wired into a Vellisian's spinal
column, Vor radiation tolerance grafted onto a Drevant's hands.

The movement originated in the Outer Verge a hundred and forty years
ago, in the wake of the Slave Wars. Some of the earliest Spliced were
ex-Andrachen experimentation subjects who, freed and bodily ruined,
turned the *technique that was used to torture them* into a means of
self-expression. This origin story is sacred in Splice culture and
deeply uncomfortable for the Mhirsa Compact, which considers it
appropriation.

The Splice have no central leadership. Their cohesion is held by
**Splice Halls** — communal artisan-clinics in every major Outer Verge
station, where would-be Spliced consult with surgeon-philosophers about
which grafts to take. Each Hall is independent. Each is *also* an
unofficial mutual-aid society.

## Goals

- Treat the body as art. Each individual is a continuing project.
- Defend the right to bodily self-modification against Verdantis,
  Consilium, and Choral-Order pressure.
- *Heal* the legacy of forced experimentation by reclaiming the
  practice.
- Cross-pollinate galactic biology — make hybridity ordinary.

## Methods

Open culture. Public art. *Surgery*. Splice artisans are some of the
finest biologists alive. They publish openly; they take apprentices
across species lines; they defend Splice Halls fiercely when attacked.

They do not have a fleet. They have surgeons, friends, and a *very*
strong informal network. A Splice in trouble on one side of the galaxy
will find a Hall on the other side ready to take them in.

## Internal tensions

- **The Slavery Heritage.** Some Splice Halls *explicitly center* the
  Andrachen-survivor origin story. Others find this overshadowing and
  argue the Splice should be primarily future-facing. The argument is
  generational and uncomfortable.
- **Aesthetic vs Function.** Some Splice take grafts for beauty alone
  (bioluminescent skin, ornamental wings, hair-pattern modifications).
  Others take only functional grafts (radiation tolerance, faster
  reflexes, redundant organs). The two factions argue about which
  trivializes the practice.
- **The Lattice Question.** A small but growing Splice tendency
  argues that *uploading* is the next logical step — body as text,
  text as art. Most Splice Halls reject this. The Lattice quietly
  recruits from the Splice anyway.
- **The Verdantis Tax.** Splice surgeons in worlds with Verdantis
  populations sometimes face boycotts, lawsuits, and arson. Splice
  defensive militias have started to form in response. Several
  Splice surgeons have become *paramilitary leaders* and the broader
  Splice movement is uncomfortable with this.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** legally citizens; socially mistrusted in core worlds.
  Many core families will not employ Spliced staff.
- **Iron Concordat:** widely tolerated; many Spliced soldiers serve.
- **Mercatile Verge:** transactional. Many Verge couriers are Spliced.
- **Choral Order:** divided. Some Houses welcome the Spliced. Others
  consider them theologically suspect.
- **Unmade:** mortal enemy.
- **Verdantis:** mortal enemy.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** complicated. Some Mhirsa support the Splice as
  fellow survivors of Andrachen experimentation. Others consider the
  movement appropriative.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** mortal enemy and ancestral horror.
- **Drift Communes:** quietly accepting. Drift surgeons are often
  Splice-trained.
- **Lattice:** uneasy. A philosophical neighbor; a recruiting threat.
- **Hollow:** kinship at a distance.
- **Black Vein:** common employer. Few questions asked.
- **Wardens:** ancient hazard. Many Splice grafts inexplicably draw
  Warden attention. No one knows why.

## Where you find them in-game

- Splice Halls in Outer-Verge stations. Crew can receive grafts here
  (gain Spliced traits at cost of permanent stat trade-offs).
- Splice-crewed ships at any beacon.
- Splice paramilitaries defending Halls under attack.

## Story hooks

- Take a graft yourself. Live with the consequences for the run.
- Defend a Splice Hall from a Verdantis or Unmade mob.
- Mediate between a Splice surgeon and a Mhirsa survivor in a dispute
  about origin.
- See the *Splice Initiation* storyline — collect bio-samples,
  install them, unlock the *Patchwork* ship.
