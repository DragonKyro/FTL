# Mhirsa Compact

> *They made us to obey. They are still surprised that we have not
> forgiven them. They will keep being surprised.* — Compact Speaker
> Ren-Hurr Vell-Tarr, third generation free-born

## Identity & origin

The Mhirsa Compact is the sovereign federation of the freed **Mhirsa** —
the ape-descended worker-soldier species engineered by House Andrachen
to be obedient, strong, short-lived, and replaceable. Mhirsa rebellions
spanned six decades; the *Slave Wars* ended a hundred and twenty years
ago with Andrachen's corporate hegemony in ruins and the surviving
Mhirsa free.

Recognition came slowly. The Consilium recognized the Compact as a
sovereign state thirty-five years after the war ended. The Iron Concordat
(then still part of the Consilium) supplied weapons and intelligence
throughout the war and has never been forgiven by the Andrachen for it,
nor entirely thanked for it by the Mhirsa.

The Compact governs through the **Council of First-Born** — Mhirsa who
were the first generation born free, now elderly, holding life seats.
When the last First-Born dies (estimated within twenty years), the
Council will transition to elected seats. Everyone Mhirsa is debating
*how*. The current First Speaker is **Karra Vell-Sann**, fifty-two, the
youngest First-Born alive.

## Goals

- Hunt down every remaining Andrachen breeder-station and breeder-line.
- Ensure no successor program (cloning, neural conditioning, indenture-
  by-debt) re-creates Mhirsa-style enslavement under another name.
- Build Mhirsa civilization beyond the war. Schools, hospitals, art,
  durable infrastructure.
- Survive the Collapse without re-becoming dependent on outsider
  patronage.
- Decide what the post-First-Born Compact will be.

## Methods

Hunter-killer fleets — the **Yokebreaker Squadrons** — that track
Andrachen breeder-stations across known space. They are small,
relentless, and operate under broad letters of authority from the
Compact's Council.

Diplomatic representation in every major faction's court. A Mhirsa
diplomat is unusual to most species' eyes (Mhirsa age fast and tend to
look young throughout an aggressive career) but unmistakably present.

Civilian construction: the Compact's worlds are visibly *new*. Every
generation builds. The wear of centuries that the Consilium and the
Verge wear like a coat — the Compact doesn't have it yet, and the
absence is striking.

## Internal tensions

- **The post-First-Born Question.** When the founders die, what
  replaces them? Some Mhirsa want pure democracy. Some want to keep
  Council seats appointed by warrior-merit. A small movement wants
  a *return to bred caste structure*, "but for our benefit, this
  time," which most Mhirsa find horrifying.
- **The Karrukai Question.** The Iron Concordat conscripts Karrukai
  as shock troops, much the way the Andrachen used Mhirsa. The
  Compact has *not* publicly condemned this and many Mhirsa are
  furious about the silence. The Council fears alienating the
  Concordat ally.
- **The Splice Question.** Some Mhirsa welcome the Splice movement
  as fellow survivors of Andrachen-style experimentation. Others
  consider Splice culture appropriative — they "play with"
  experimentation as art, while Mhirsa lived its terror.
- **Hunter excesses.** Yokebreaker Squadrons have, on at least four
  documented occasions, killed Andrachen civilians along with their
  targets. The Compact has not officially apologized. Some captains
  resign over it. Some recruit on it.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** allied; sometimes resented for the slow recognition.
- **Iron Concordat:** old comrade in the Slave Wars; modern friction
  over the Karrukai program.
- **Mercatile Verge:** transactional; the Compact runs heavily on
  Verge-shipped construction materials.
- **Choral Order:** allied; the Order sided with the Mhirsa during
  the Slave Wars and was almost extinguished in Andrachen reprisals
  for it.
- **Unmade:** hated. The Unmade have bombed Mhirsa schools.
- **Verdantis:** allied against Andrachen heritage.
- **Splice:** complicated, see above.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** existential enemy.
- **Drift Communes:** some Communes sheltered Mhirsa rebels during
  the war; alliance runs deep in those Communes specifically.
- **Lattice:** unrecognized as a political entity. Privately feared
  as "the same kind of thing the Andrachen tried to make."
- **Hollow:** mistrusted by reflex.
- **Black Vein:** mortal enemy. Slave-running crews are *the*
  Yokebreaker priority target.
- **Wardens / Outerlight:** apocalyptic background hazard.

## Where you find them in-game

- Yokebreaker patrols in any sector with confirmed Andrachen activity.
- Mhirsa worlds — visibly new construction, schools, museums of the
  Slave Wars.
- Mhirsa-run free-trade hubs that *aggressively* exclude Andrachen-
  affiliated traffic.

## Story hooks

- Take a Yokebreaker Squadron commission against a breeder-station —
  and decide what to do about the children on board.
- Be offered Mhirsa diplomatic immunity in exchange for evidence on
  Andrachen networks.
- Mediate the Splice / Mhirsa dispute on a shared station.
- See the *Mhirsa Liberation* storyline — full hunter playthrough,
  unlocks the *Yoke-Breaker* ship.
