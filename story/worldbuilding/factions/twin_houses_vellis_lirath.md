# Twin Houses of Vellis and Lirath

> *They were going to be married. The marriage was forbidden. They had
> the child anyway. Now everyone is dying for it, including the child.
> Especially the child.* — historian Tovin Renn-Vass

## Identity & origin

Vassirin is a single, wealthy, mid-Spiral world ruled jointly by two
ancient aristocratic houses — **Vellis** and **Lirath**. The houses
share a planet, share a throne, share a court, share a language, and
share four thousand years of feud and reconciliation.

The compact that holds them together was sealed two hundred years ago
by a marriage: when Vellis and Lirath interbreed, the throne is
stable. When they don't, civil war resumes. The compact specifies
which generations may marry across, on what schedule, by which rites.

Forty years ago, two young aristocrats — **Hannar Vellis-Tahn** and
**Eldress Lirath-Mor** — fell in love at a time when *no cross-marriage
was permitted*. They married in secret. They had a child. The houses
learned. Both houses denounced the marriage and disowned the parents
and the child. The parents went into hiding. Both later died — Hannar
in a duel with a Vellis cousin, Eldress in a "hunting accident" everyone
on the planet knows was a Lirath knife.

The child survived. The child is now forty years old, a starship
captain in the Outer Verge, and is the reason both houses are at war.

That child is the **Tainted Heir** — Captain **Iolen Vellis-Lirath**,
known by no one as their full name and by both houses as *the abomination
that broke the compact*. Iolen captains a ship called *Half-Crown*,
holds no fixed allegiance, takes mercenary contracts across the galaxy,
and is a brilliant tactician who is, by every account from those who
have served with them, a slow-motion wreck of a person.

## Goals

**House Vellis** wants:

- To capture Iolen alive (preferred) and *re-legitimize them* as a
  Vellis-only heir, voiding the Lirath claim, taking the throne.
- Or, failing that, to kill Iolen, voiding the Lirath claim, taking
  the throne.

**House Lirath** wants:

- The same, but Lirath.

**Iolen Vellis-Lirath** wants:

- To stop being the reason for any of it.
- They have, three times, attempted to publicly renounce all claim
  to the throne. Both houses have refused to accept the renunciation,
  on the grounds that the *very existence* of a cross-house heir is
  the destabilizing factor, not the heir's claim.

## Methods

**House Vellis** funds the **Crown's Lance** mercenary company, runs
intelligence operations through Mercatile Verge contracts, and quietly
purchases bounty contracts on Iolen through Black Vein intermediaries.

**House Lirath** funds the **White Cipher** mercenary company, runs
intelligence operations through Choral Order contacts (controversially),
and quietly purchases bounty contracts through different Black Vein
intermediaries.

Both houses are *deeply* embarrassed by their own willingness to use
the Black Vein. Both deny it. Both continue to do it.

**Captain Iolen** survives on tactical skill, paranoia, and a *very*
unusual crew that includes both a Vellisian *and* a Lirathi (the only
mixed-house crew currently known to operate). The crew is loyal because
Iolen is the captain who *makes the impossible work* — they win
contracts they should not win, escape ambushes they should not escape,
and pay better than either house.

## Internal tensions

- **Within Vellis.** A growing reform faction wants to *accept the
  compact's failure*, abolish the joint throne, and declare Vellis
  sovereignty over half of Vassirin. The traditional faction wants
  the throne.
- **Within Lirath.** The mirror argument, with the same proportions.
- **Within Iolen's crew.** The Vellisian crew member and the Lirathi
  crew member are *both* in love with Iolen. Iolen knows. Iolen does
  not respond. The triangle has been stable, painfully, for eleven
  years.

## Stance toward other factions

This conflict is *almost entirely internal*. It does, however, spill:

- **Consilium:** technically presides over Vassirin under the old
  Compact. Has been *unable* to mediate for forty years.
- **Iron Concordat:** Vassirin is on the Concordat side of the Inner
  Spiral / Outer Verge line, technically. Neither house has fully
  endorsed Concordat secession; both *quietly* take Concordat money.
- **Choral Order:** asked to mediate; politely declined; privately
  embarrassed.
- **Mercatile Verge:** profits from the war.
- **Black Vein:** profits from the war. Chair of Chairs **Korrin Vell**
  is herself a Vellisian defector and has *publicly refused* to take
  contracts from either house. Both houses have placed bounties on her.
- **Splice:** Iolen has been Spliced (one trait, Vor radiation
  tolerance) for tactical reasons. Both houses cite this as proof of
  Iolen's degeneracy.
- **Verdantis:** does not interfere.
- All other factions: not directly involved.

## Where you find them in-game

- Vassirin itself — beautiful, layered, gorgeous architecture, sectors
  that toggle between Vellis and Lirath control beacon by beacon.
- Mercenary companies (Crown's Lance, White Cipher) offering contracts
  in any sector.
- *Captain Iolen and the Half-Crown* as a rare encounter — once per
  storyline.
- Random ambushes in any sector where the player has accepted a Vellis
  or Lirath contract and the other house's hunters have caught up.

## Story hooks

- Take a Vellis contract. Take a Lirath contract. Discover both
  contracts target the same person.
- Be approached, in the Outer Verge, by Captain Iolen for a mutual-
  protection arrangement.
- Witness a reconciliation attempt fail spectacularly and have to
  pick a side mid-event.
- See the *Vellis-Lirath Tragedy* storyline — two endings depending
  on choice, unlocks one of two house-themed ships, plus a wildcard
  crewmate (Iolen themselves) on the "broker peace" path.
