# Mercatile Verge

> *The Gate is open. The toll is fair. The toll is the toll. Move along,
> captain.* — script taught to every Verge tollkeeper

## Identity & origin

The Mercatile Verge is the merchant guild that controls every working
Helix Gate in surveyed space. They did not build the Gates — the Vyrnari
did — but they built the *infrastructure around them*: the toll stations,
the customs houses, the artificial habitats, the docking arrays, the
escrow systems, the bonded couriers, the price-clearance archives.

The Verge began four centuries ago as a courier cooperative. It became
a guild. It became a near-monopoly. It became, depending whom you ask,
either the *vascular system* of the galaxy or its *parasite*.

Its leadership is the **Council of Ledgers** — nine families,
multi-species but Sapien-dominated, who control toll-rate setting,
customs policy, and inter-Verge dispute resolution. Real authority shifts
constantly among them; the family that *runs* the Verge in any given
decade is the one whose ledgers balance best.

## Goals

- Keep the working Gates open. Their entire business model depends on it.
- Maintain toll authority. Resist all attempts (Consilium, Concordat,
  Lattice) to nationalize, expropriate, or bypass the Gates.
- Survive the Collapse by raising tolls only as much as the market will
  bear before it shatters.
- Quietly research *what is making the Gates fail*, with a view to
  fixing or bribing it.

## Methods

Tolls. Customs duties. Information asymmetry. Bonded escrow. The Verge
*never* runs hostile fleets; instead, when a faction or smuggler ring
needs to be punished, the Verge contracts privateers (often Black Vein,
embarrassingly) to do the work.

The Verge's *most dangerous tool* is information: they know who shipped
what to whom across every Gate they control. They sell that information,
selectively, to whoever pays best. Many wars have started because a Verge
ledger leaked.

## Internal tensions

- **The Sealed-Gate Crisis.** The Verge has lost roughly a fifth of its
  toll-revenue to sealed Gates. Some families want to raise rates on
  the surviving Gates to compensate. Others fear the rates are already
  near a breaking point.
- **The Indentured-Labor Toll.** Several families have begun accepting
  indentured labor as toll-payment from poor colonies. The other
  families consider this a moral and political catastrophe. It hasn't
  been stopped.
- **The Council of Ledgers' Halene Faction.** A small Halene-led bloc
  argues the Verge should *give up Gates that are likely to fail*
  proactively, to stop being blamed for losses they don't cause. Most
  families consider this surrender.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** uneasy partner. Pays tribute under treaty. The
  Consilium would love to nationalize the Gates and cannot.
- **Iron Concordat:** transactional. Pays the toll, but slightly
  cheaper, by treaty. Resented.
- **Choral Order:** Verge stations house Choral shrines for crew
  morale and tourism.
- **Unmade:** designated terrorist; Verge bounties are very high.
- **Verdantis:** quiet but profitable trade routes.
- **Splice:** legal.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** correct, profitable.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** denounced publicly; *certain Verge families
  have private accounts with them*.
- **Drift Communes:** historically irrelevant. Lately a major worry —
  Communes are bypassing the Verge entirely with their old jump-tug
  routes.
- **Lattice:** Verge does not, as policy, do business with the Lattice.
  A few families do anyway.
- **Hollow:** legally persons; charged the same tolls as anyone.
- **Black Vein Syndicate:** mortal enemy in public, sometimes-contractor
  in private, eternal pretextual nemesis in both.
- **Wardens:** the Verge's worst-kept secret is that they *try* to
  cooperate with Warden activity. Sometimes the Wardens accept.

## Where you find them in-game

- Every working Gate has a Verge toll station.
- Free-port habitats orbiting major Gates, full of legal and illegal
  business.
- Auctions for salvaged Vyrnari tech, openly bid.
- Customs inspections — random, sometimes adversarial.

## Story hooks

- Smuggle goods past Verge customs and decide what to do when caught.
- Take a Verge privateer commission against the Black Vein — they pay
  in scrap *and* market-info coupons.
- Investigate the indentured-labor tolls scandal: who's profiting?
- Be the captain who *bypasses* the Verge by chartering a Drift
  Commune tug. See the *Drift Commune Way* storyline.
