# The Outerlight

> *I saw it through the failing Gate. It was beautiful. It was a kind
> of beauty that should not happen to a person. The hand I am writing
> this with is not the hand I had yesterday.* — last logged entry from
> the freighter *Sansera*, found derelict, crew missing.

## Identity & origin

The Outerlight is *the thing*.

Nobody knows what it is. Nobody knows where it is. Different factions
believe it is:

- An entity (or entities) the Vyrnari sealed *away* from the galaxy
  ten thousand years ago. *(Choral Order, most mainstream interpretation.)*
- The Vyrnari themselves, returning. *(Some Choral splinters; some
  Unmade; some Lattice threads.)*
- The Vyrnari themselves, *malfunctioning*, returning as something
  that no longer matches what they were. *(Lattice fringe theory.)*
- A natural phenomenon — some subspace fauna, some pre-galactic
  Outside, some collapse of the substrate the Gates ride on. *(Iron
  Concordat technical bureau.)*
- A *mass psychosis* induced by Gate-failure radiation. *(Consilium
  technical bureau; not seriously believed by anyone in the field.)*
- *Us.* The thing we will become, reaching back through time. *(One
  particularly grim Whisperborn fragment, considered heresy by both
  Choral Order and Unmade.)*

These interpretations are *not* equally supported. They are also not
falsifiable, because the Outerlight does not respond to questions.

## What is observed

The Outerlight manifests near *failing* Helix Gates. Most Gate
failures are silent and uneventful. A small fraction — perhaps one in
twenty — produce phenomena before the Gate fully seals:

- **Visual.** A light "that is not light" — observers describe it as
  *seeing without illumination*, or as *light traveling at the wrong
  speed*. Recordings of the phenomenon are static-laced and disagree
  with each other's color.
- **Auditory / Echo-channel.** Choir, Whisperborn, and some Yssari
  report hearing *something attentive* — not a voice, not a sound,
  but an awareness pressing against their channel. Most cannot stand
  it for more than seconds.
- **Effect on ships.** Ships that approach a failing Gate during an
  Outerlight event have, on documented occasions:
  - Returned with their crews *intact-but-empty* (catatonic, sometimes
    permanently).
  - Returned with their crews *replaced* — same bodies, different
    behaviors, no memory of the originals' lives.
  - Returned with one or two extra crew members who were not on the
    manifest and cannot be photographed.
  - Returned without their hulls.
  - Not returned at all.

## What appears to be true

- The Outerlight is *responsive* — it reacts to who and what
  approaches it. It is not a passive natural phenomenon.
- The Outerlight is *not always hostile* — some encounters end with
  the affected ship returning intact and unharmed. The pattern is
  not understood.
- The Outerlight is *increasing*. The rate of Outerlight-active
  Gate failures has doubled in the last twelve years.
- Wardens hate the Outerlight, or are the Outerlight, or both. (Yes.
  Both possibilities exist in the field literature.)

## Goals

Inferred only:

- *To get through.* Whatever it is, it acts as if it is *coming from
  outside the galaxy* and *trying to enter*. Every failing Gate is
  one fewer barrier.
- Possibly: *to communicate*. The Echo-channel evidence suggests
  *attentiveness* — something watching back, choosing.
- Possibly: nothing. Possibly it is a process, not an agent, and
  ascribing goals to it is the species' habit.

## Stance toward other factions

The Outerlight does not have a stance. Or it has all stances. Or it
has stances toward individual *people* and not toward groups.

What is true: *every Echo-listener who hears the Outerlight clearly
becomes unfit for normal life within a year, on average*. The Choral
Order's standard pastoral response to "I think I heard the Outerlight"
is to immediately quarantine the listener in a Chorus House and pray.

## Where you find them in-game

The Outerlight is **not normally an encounter type**. The player will
not "fight an Outerlight ship" in a normal sector. The Outerlight is:

- A reason a sector goes dark on the star map.
- A reason a Warden is in a sector at all.
- A *narrative event* on certain storylines (Choral, Unmade,
  Whisperborn) where the player witnesses an Outerlight Gate-failure
  in person and must choose how to respond.
- The *final boss of the Whisperborn and Unmade storylines*, where
  the player either fights the Outerlight in a way no other faction
  can, *lets it through*, or *bargains with it* (Whisperborn-only).

## Story hooks

- Witness an Outerlight event from a safe distance, and lose a crew
  member to it who looked too long.
- Investigate a derelict found in a once-Outerlight-active region.
- See the *Whisperborn Awakening* storyline for the only path that
  ends in direct, narrative engagement with what the Outerlight may
  be.
- See the *Unmade Heresy* storyline for the dark path that *welcomes*
  the Outerlight in.
