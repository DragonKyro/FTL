# Black Vein Syndicate

> *The Verge says we are criminals. The Verge is correct. The Verge is
> not, however, the only kind of person worth being.* — attributed to
> Captain Saskia "Long-Hand" Kerrin, two weeks before her hanging

## Identity & origin

The Black Vein is the loose confederation of pirates, smugglers, salvors,
relic-dealers, and outright slavers operating outside Mercatile Verge
authority. They are not a single organization. They are perhaps fifty
crews, half a dozen *Cartel Chairs* who broker between crews, and a
dense network of black-market fences across every habitable sector.

They take their name from the **Black Vein routes** — old, dangerous,
mostly-uncharted void corridors that bypass Verge-controlled Gates.
These corridors include partial-Gates, slow conduits, and risky long-
burns through deep void.

There is no central leader. The closest thing is the **Chair of Chairs**,
a courtesy title held by whichever Cartel Chair has the most leverage
that quarter. Currently held by **Korrin Vell**, a Vellisian defector
(yes, *that* Vellisian — see the Vellis-Lirath storyline).

## Goals

- Make money.
- Stay alive.
- Avoid centralizing into anything the Verge can target.
- Acquire and sell Vyrnari relics — the most lucrative cargo in known
  space.
- Survive the Collapse better than the Verge does. (Many Cartel Chairs
  believe they will.)

## Methods

Piracy. Smuggling. Salvage from sealed-Gate wreckage. Vyrnari relic
trade. Bonded slave labor (a minority of crews; the Chairs publicly
disavow it; privately it persists). Information brokerage to the
Andrachen-in-Exile and occasionally to the Ninth Bureau (which is how
the Ninth Bureau prefers it).

A Black Vein crew that gets too big becomes a target for every faction
at once. A crew that stays small can run for years.

## Internal tensions

- **The Slavery Question.** Two of the six current Cartel Chairs run
  crews that traffic in indentured labor or worse. Three publicly
  oppose this. One is silent. The Chair of Chairs is *furious* about
  it because the slavers attract Mhirsa Compact hunter-fleets, which
  is bad for everyone's business.
- **The Andrachen Money.** Same as the Concordat's Andrachen problem,
  but more open. Several Cartel Chairs are directly Andrachen-funded.
- **Relic vs Cargo.** Some crews run profitable, low-risk smuggling
  routes. Some chase Vyrnari relics that draw Quarantine Warden
  attention. The relic-runners are richer and shorter-lived.
- **Korrin Vell's identity.** A Vellisian Chair of Chairs has destabilized
  the Vellis-Lirath civil war by *not picking a side*. Both houses have
  put bounties on her. She doesn't care; the bounties are good marketing.

## Stance toward other factions

- **Consilium:** mortal enemy in public. Trades information with the
  Ninth Bureau in private.
- **Iron Concordat:** the Concordat shoots Black Vein crews on sight.
  Some crews shoot back. Some don't.
- **Mercatile Verge:** mortal enemy. Many crews are former Verge
  couriers who quit when toll rates rose.
- **Choral Order:** generally hands-off. Some crews donate relic finds
  to Choral shrines to acquire merit. Mostly cosmetic.
- **Unmade:** avoided. The Unmade do not pay in coin.
- **Verdantis:** rarely interacts. Verdantis trade routes are not
  worth the risk.
- **Splice:** common among crews. The Black Vein doesn't care what
  your body looks like as long as you pull your share.
- **Mhirsa Compact:** *terrified* of Mhirsa hunter-fleets. The
  slavery-affiliated crews especially.
- **Andrachen-in-Exile:** investors and clients.
- **Drift Communes:** sometimes-allies on Black Vein routes; the
  Communes will charter to anyone.
- **Lattice / Hollow:** awkward. Some crews use Hollow as off-the-books
  navigators because Hollow don't need life support.
- **Wardens:** *the* central professional hazard.

## Where you find them in-game

- Off-Gate void routes (Black Vein corridors).
- Black markets in Verge free-ports.
- Encounters with pirate ships at any beacon, but especially in
  unpoliced or contested sectors.
- Salvage fields, often with multiple pirate factions competing.

## Story hooks

- Be hired by a Cartel Chair to run cargo whose contents you're not
  allowed to know.
- Ambush a Black Vein slave-runner and free its hold (Mhirsa rep
  *very* positive; Black Vein rep negative).
- Acquire a Vyrnari relic from a Black Vein dealer and learn what it
  was meant to do.
- Meet Korrin Vell and discover she'd like an arrangement.
- See the *Black Vein Run* storyline — full pirate playthrough.
